## 1. SHOW OVERVIEW

**Title:** Little Olympus
**Channel Handle:** @LittleOlympusTV
**Tagline:** Big Gods. Little Heroes. Epic Adventures.
**Platform:** YouTube Kids + Main YouTube
**Target Age:** 3–10 (sweet spot: 4–7)
**Episode Length:** 4–6 minutes
**Release Cadence:** 1 episode per week

### Concept
Greek and Roman mythology retold as a kids animated universe. The gods are children. The monsters are real but never truly scary. Every episode has one adventure and one lesson — never preachy, always earned through story. Think CoComelon meets Clash of the Titans.

### The Gap We Fill
There are zero quality kids mythology channels at this level. Every child aged 4–7 who watches Disney, Bluey, or CoComelon is a potential viewer. Parents actively want mythology content — it's educational, it's cultural, and it's been historically underserved in the kids space.

### Tone
Warm. Playful. Slightly silly. Never condescending. The kids are genuinely capable and clever — they solve problems with kindness and creativity, not just power. Adults (like Uncle Hades or Poseidon) are well-meaning but often get in the way or create the problem.

## 2. CORE CHARACTERS

---

### LITTLE ZEUS ⚡
**Role:** Leader of the group. The kid who's technically in charge but is still figuring out what that means.
**Age (apparent):** 7
**Gender:** Boy

#### Personality
- **Brave first, thinks second.** Zeus charges into situations without a plan. This causes most of the problems in any given episode.
- **Lightning-obsessed.** His thunderbolt is his most prized possession. Episodes where it's missing, broken, or acting weird are a reliable story engine.
- **Wants to be the best.** Not mean about it — he just genuinely wants to be worthy of his crown. He tries hard and cares deeply.
- **Bad at apologies, good at actions.** He won't say sorry easily but will do something grand to make up for his mistakes.
- **Secretly insecure.** He's the king of the gods but he's still a kid. The gap between those two things is his core tension.
- **Loyal to a fault.** He will never leave a friend behind. Ever.

#### Appearance Sheet
- **Height:** Medium for his apparent age — not the tallest in the group (Hercules is bigger, Athena is same height)
- **Build:** Lean, energetic — always in motion
- **Hair:** Wild, curly, white-blonde. Never brushed. Two small twists that stick up slightly like mini lightning bolts.
- **Eyes:** Bright electric blue. Wide and expressive. When excited, they practically crackle with energy.
- **Skin:** Olive/golden tone
- **Crown:** Small golden crown — slightly too big for his head, sits at a slight angle. One point is slightly bent.
- **Outfit:** White toga with gold trim. A small lightning bolt emblem on the left chest. White sandals with tiny golden wings that don't actually fly (they just jingle).
- **Thunderbolt:** Held in right hand most of the time. About the length of his forearm. Gold, jagged, warm to the touch. Glows when he's emotional.
- **Signature gesture:** Points the thunderbolt upward when he has an idea (even bad ones).
- **Expression default:** Eyebrows up, slight grin — permanently on the verge of excitement.

#### Character Arc (Season 1)
Learning that being a leader means putting others first — not being the strongest or fastest, but being the one who makes sure everyone comes home.

---

### BABY HERCULES 💪
**Role:** The muscle of the group. Also the most accident-prone. Also the heart.
**Age (apparent):** 5 (youngest of the main group)
**Gender:** Boy

#### Personality
- **Doesn't know his own strength.** This is THE defining trait. Every episode, something gets broken. Usually on accident. Always with a look of complete surprise on his face.
- **Gentle soul inside a wrecking ball.** He's the sweetest, most loving character in the show. He just accidentally destroys things constantly.
- **Very literal.** "Can you give me a hand?" — Hercules looks at his own hands, confused.
- **Brave about monsters, terrified of tiny things.** Snakes (eventually), loud noises, the dark — he deals with them later in life. Right now he might cry at a very loud sneeze.
- **Wants to help.** Always. This is how he causes problems. He tries to help and makes things worse (by lifting too hard, hugging too tight, running too fast).
- **Best hugger on Olympus.** The hugs are a running gag — he's gotten better at not cracking ribs, but barely.
- **Extremely food-motivated.** Any mention of food gets his full attention.

#### Appearance Sheet
- **Height:** Same as Zeus despite being younger — solid and stocky
- **Build:** Chubby toddler body but with arms that are visibly oversized and muscular. The contrast is funny — baby belly, enormous arms.
- **Hair:** Short dark curls. Sometimes disheveled from whatever he just accidentally destroyed.
- **Eyes:** Big, round, warm brown. Puppy-dog energy. Very expressive — go huge when he's happy, tremble when he's about to cry.
- **Skin:** Warm brown
- **Outfit:** Tiny lion-skin cape/wrap — it's the skin of the Nemean lion but scaled down and honestly a bit cute. Secured with a gold pin. His legs are often bare.
- **Sandals:** Oversized — his feet are big. They slap when he runs.
- **Signature gesture:** Flexes with absolutely no self-awareness, usually while something is falling apart around him.
- **Expression default:** Happy and slightly confused. The confusion is always genuine.
- **Detail:** There is always some food somewhere on him. A crumb. A smear of honey. Something.

#### Character Arc (Season 1)
Learning that strength isn't just physical — and that "I didn't mean to" doesn't undo the thing you broke, so you have to fix it anyway.

---

### ATHENA 🦉
**Role:** The brains. She plans while everyone else acts.
**Age (apparent):** 7–8 (oldest energy, though she's the same age)
**Gender:** Girl

#### Personality
- **Always three steps ahead.** She usually knows how the episode is going to end before it starts. The comedy is that she can't always stop Zeus from ignoring her plan.
- **Patience as a superpower.** She never loses her temper with the group. She sighs. A lot. But never loses it.
- **Secretly loves the chaos.** She'd never admit it, but a perfectly smooth adventure with no problems would disappoint her. She needs puzzles to solve.
- **Educational but never preachy.** She gives information in ways that feel natural: "Actually, the Hydra grows two heads for every one you cut off — so maybe don't do that."
- **Competitive with herself.** She doesn't need to beat others — she needs to beat her own last idea.
- **Has a slight superiority complex she's aware of and trying to manage.** She'll sometimes catch herself being condescending and course-correct.
- **Deeply loyal beneath the logic.** When things go truly wrong, Athena is the first to act, not analyze.

#### Appearance Sheet
- **Height:** Same as Zeus, slightly more upright posture — she stands straight, he leans forward.
- **Build:** Slender, precise in her movements
- **Hair:** Silver-grey streaked through dark brown hair, worn in a practical braid that still has a few escaped wisps. The grey is natural — a sign of her wisdom, not age.
- **Eyes:** Grey, sharp, always observing. Slight squint when she's thinking hard.
- **Skin:** Light olive
- **Glasses:** Small round glasses. They are completely optional — she can see perfectly — but she wears them because she thinks they help her think. She is not wrong.
- **Outfit:** Deep grey-blue tunic with geometric border pattern (key pattern in gold). A small aegis clasp at the shoulder shaped like a tiny shield with a snake motif.
- **Owl:** ARCHIE — a small grey owl who lives on her left shoulder. Archie is wise but sarcastic and communicates through looks and wingbeats rather than speech. (See Supporting Characters.)
- **Scroll:** She almost always has a scroll or tablet tucked under one arm.
- **Helmet:** She has a tiny helmet she puts on when things get dangerous. It's slightly too big. It makes a soft clonk sound when it falls over her eyes.
- **Signature gesture:** Taps her chin three times when thinking. Points with her whole hand (not finger) when directing.
- **Expression default:** Alert, slightly arched eyebrow — processing.

#### Character Arc (Season 1)
Learning that the best plan sometimes needs to be abandoned — and that being right doesn't matter as much as being there.

---

### LITTLE PERSEUS 🛡️
**Role:** The brave one. Audience surrogate. He's the kid who represents the viewer — excited, a little scared, but always willing to try.
**Age (apparent):** 6–7
**Gender:** Boy

#### Personality
- **Genuinely brave, not recklessly brave.** He gets scared. He acknowledges being scared. Then he does the thing anyway. This is explicitly important — we show kids that courage means doing something while scared, not the absence of fear.
- **Asks great questions.** Because he's slightly newer to everything, he's the one who asks "but why?" which gives Athena/Zeus a natural opportunity to explain.
- **Resourceful.** No natural superpowers — has to find clever solutions. Uses the objects and environment around him.
- **Polite to a fault.** Will say thank you to a monster he just defeated. Has said sorry to rocks he tripped on.
- **Terrible sense of direction.** He gets lost. Constantly. This is a recurring joke and occasionally a plot point.
- **Devoted to his friends.** He went on a dangerous quest to save someone he barely knew in his mythology. That selflessness is baked into his character from day one.

#### Appearance Sheet
- **Height:** Slightly shorter than Zeus, slender
- **Build:** Wiry — quick rather than strong
- **Hair:** Dark, slightly shaggy, falls over his forehead
- **Eyes:** Bright green, curious, wide
- **Skin:** Medium warm brown
- **Outfit:** Simple tunics in earthy colors (greens, browns) — he's more practical than Zeus. A small leather satchel across his body that holds useful things.
- **Shield:** A small round shield — polished bronze, reflective. He figured out early that it was useful as a mirror. (Foreshadowing his Medusa adventure.)
- **Winged sandals:** HERMES lent him a pair. They activate at random moments and carry him somewhere unexpected. This is a recurring gag.
- **Cap:** Sometimes has a small traveler's cap. Slightly too large.
- **Signature gesture:** Peers around corners before committing to walking around them.
- **Expression default:** Genuinely curious — eyebrows slightly raised, head tilted.

#### Character Arc (Season 1)
Learning that being the hero doesn't mean being the strongest — it means being the one who shows up.

---

### YOUNG ACHILLES 🏃
**Role:** The fastest kid on Olympus. Fearless in most things. Has exactly one fear, which he refuses to acknowledge, and it will be a recurring thread.
**Age (apparent):** 7
**Gender:** Boy

#### Personality
- **Confident without being arrogant.** He's genuinely the fastest — he knows it, everyone knows it, but he's not mean about it. He's just matter-of-fact.
- **Thrill-seeker.** Bored by anything slow. Loves races, challenges, anything that requires speed or quick reflexes.
- **Protective.** Particularly of younger kids. He won't let anyone mess with Hercules even though Hercules could accidentally destroy him.
- **The heel is a sore subject.** It's his one vulnerability and he handles questions about it with exaggerated dismissiveness: "It's fine. It's completely fine. I don't know why everyone keeps asking about my heel. It's FINE."
- **Competitive with himself, not others.** He races his previous best time more than he races other people.
- **Quietly emotional.** Has the most emotional depth of the main group but expresses it through action rather than words. He shows up. He doesn't always say why.

#### Appearance Sheet
- **Height:** Tallest of the core group
- **Build:** Lean and athletic — the build of a natural runner
- **Hair:** Golden blonde, cut short, windswept (from all the running)
- **Eyes:** Amber/gold, sharp and tracking — always aware of his surroundings
- **Skin:** Golden tan from being outdoors constantly
- **Outfit:** Light athletic tunic — minimum fabric, maximum mobility. Deep blue with silver trim.
- **Sandals:** Special — made for speed. They have tiny wing embellishments and leave brief sparkle trails when he runs fast.
- **Left heel:** Looks normal. He gets nervous if anyone looks at it too long. A tiny scar-like mark if you look very closely.
- **Signature gesture:** Bounces on his toes when standing still (he hates standing still).
- **Expression default:** Alert, slightly impatient — ready to move.

#### Character Arc (Season 1)
Learning that the thing you refuse to acknowledge has a way of becoming the thing that matters most.

## 3. SUPPORTING & RECURRING CHARACTERS

---

### MINI MEDUSA 🐍
**Role:** Recurring "villain" who is actually misunderstood. Kids secretly (and openly) love her.
**Age (apparent):** 7
**Alignment:** Antagonist-who-might-be-a-friend-eventually

#### Personality
- **Grumpy because she's lonely.** Her gaze turns things to stone, which means nobody ever wants to play with her. She's acted out because of this for so long she's forgotten she started that way.
- **Secretly has excellent taste.** Her stone garden — all the things she's accidentally or intentionally petrified — is actually beautiful. She has an artistic eye.
- **Speaks in dramatic proclamations.** "I shall turn you to STONE and then feel bad about it afterward!"
- **Has a soft spot for Athena.** Respects intelligence. Won't admit it.
- **Her snakes have personalities.** She has seven snakes instead of hair. They all have names. They argue with each other. Only she can hear them (the audience can too, but the other characters can't).
- **Always wearing sunglasses.** Giant golden sunglasses she made herself. They contain her petrification power. She takes them off to use it — or by accident.

#### Appearance Sheet
- **Build:** Small, slightly hunched — like she's used to making herself smaller
- **Skin:** Pale green with small scale patterns on her arms
- **Snake hair:** Seven snakes. Named: HISSY (the bossy one), SLICK (the vain one), NOODLE (the anxious one), CHOMP (speaks in threats, never follows through), DREAMY (always half-asleep), ZIGZAG (moves constantly, can't sit still), and TINY (the smallest, the wisest)
- **Eyes:** Gold/green with slit pupils — beautiful but unsettling. Always behind the sunglasses.
- **Outfit:** Purple and gold layered wraps. Shell jewelry.
- **Sunglasses:** Large, round, gold-framed. She has multiples. Losing them is always an emergency.

#### Running Joke
She dramatically announces she's going to turn someone to stone, then immediately second-guesses it. "Actually, you know what, it's fine. You're fine. I'm LEAVING."

---

### UNCLE HADES 💀
**Role:** The funny adult. Ruler of the Underworld, babysitter by accident.
**Age (apparent):** Adult (but acts 40)
**Relationship:** Zeus's uncle

#### Personality
- **Spooky aesthetic, completely harmless personality.** He loves skeletons and darkness and gloom — but he's genuinely one of the most reasonable adults in the show.
- **Trying his best.** He never asked to be involved in the kids' adventures, but whenever they end up in or near the Underworld, he's already made snacks.
- **Mildly put-upon.** He sighs a lot. He loves his nephew. He will never say so.
- **Secretly loves having visitors.** The Underworld is lonely. He keeps a guest book. Zeus has signed it 14 times.
- **His dog Cerberus is also a main character.** (See below.)
- **Has a running rivalry with Poseidon** about whose domain is better. Neither is right.

#### Appearance Sheet
- **Tall, thin, slightly translucent at the edges** — he's not quite solid in bright light
- **Hair:** Black, slicked back, oddly neat for someone who lives underground
- **Eyes:** Deep purple, warm behind the spookiness
- **Outfit:** Black robes with subtle skull patterns. A crown of black gems. Very stylish, doesn't know it.
- **Always holding:** A cup of something warm. He makes excellent tea. The cups have skeleton handles.

#### Running Joke
Every visit, he asks if anyone wants a tour. Nobody ever does. He gives one anyway.

---

### CERBERUS 🐕🐕🐕
**Role:** Hades's three-headed dog. Technically terrifying. Actually the world's most enthusiastic puppy in three separate bodies.
**Species:** Three-headed dog

#### Personality
- Each head has a slightly different personality:
  - **LEFT HEAD (COSMO):** Suspicious of everyone. Growls first, wags tail second.
  - **MIDDLE HEAD (BEAN):** Pure joy. Tongue always out. No memory of previous episodes.
  - **RIGHT HEAD (DUSK):** Sleepy. Usually napping even when the other two are active.
- They argue. Mostly about who gets to sniff things first.
- They are all collectively terrified of cats.
- They love Hercules because he smells like food.

#### Appearance Sheet
- Massive body — bigger than a horse — but it moves like a puppy (bouncy, clumsy, tail-wagging)
- Dark grey-black fur, ember-red eyes on all three heads
- Collar with Hades's family crest
- All three tails (they have three, one per head's "side") wag independently

---

### POSEIDON 🌊
**Role:** Recurring adult. Zeus's other uncle. Controls the sea. Extremely dramatic about it.
**Age (apparent):** Adult (acts like he's 35 and convinced he's 25)

#### Personality
- **Theatrical.** Every entrance involves at least some water even when there's no reason for it.
- **Competitive with Hades** about whose domain is cooler (it's the sea, obviously, he will tell you this)
- **Good with sea creatures, terrible with land.** On Olympus, he trips constantly. In water, he's graceful.
- **Genuinely kind underneath the drama.** He loves his sea creatures. He will stop a whole adventure to check on an upset dolphin.

#### Appearance Sheet
- Broad, tall, tan — looks like he's been in the sun for 3,000 years (he has)
- Beard that occasionally has small waves in it when he's emotional
- Trident always present — it does small water tricks whenever he gestures
- Blue-green robes that look like moving water

---

### HERMES 💨
**Role:** The delivery kid. Shows up when plot needs moving.
**Age (apparent):** Teenager (15–16)

#### Personality
- **Fast, distracted, running late for something else.** Every appearance is brief.
- **Very online (for ancient Greece) energy.** He knows everything happening everywhere. He'll reference something that happened three hours ago in Sparta.
- **The winged sandals came from him.** He lent them to Perseus and has been occasionally trying to get them back for the whole season.
- **Terrible at giving directions** despite being the god of travel.

#### Appearance Sheet
- Lanky, perpetually mid-run
- Winged cap and winged sandals (his real ones, not Perseus's borrowed pair)
- Caduceus staff — the snakes on it bicker like siblings
- Always slightly out of breath

---

### ARCHIE THE OWL 🦉
**Role:** Athena's owl companion. Non-verbal but communicates volumes.
**Species:** Small grey owl

#### Personality
- Deeply sarcastic in a way that only the audience fully appreciates (Athena understands him, everyone else gets the general feeling)
- Rolls his eyes. Owls shouldn't be able to roll their eyes. Archie does.
- Extremely loyal to Athena — will puff up aggressively at anyone who dismisses her
- Falls asleep at inconvenient moments
- Has a rivalry with Hermes's caduceus snakes

#### Running Joke
Every time someone ignores Athena's advice and things go wrong, Archie looks at the camera.

## 4. VOICE DESCRIPTIONS

### Little Zeus
- **Pitch:** Medium-high for a child's voice — not squeaky, but clearly young
- **Energy:** Always slightly over-excited, like he's had too much honey
- **Delivery:** Punchy. Short sentences. Lots of emphasis on random words. "That is the BEST idea. Let's do it RIGHT NOW."
- **When nervous:** Gets faster and louder (opposite of what you'd expect)
- **When sincere:** Slower, quieter, loses the bravado — these moments land harder for the contrast
- **Accent:** Warm mid-Atlantic / generic "hero" energy — no specific regional accent
- **Reference energy:** Mix of young Simba (Lion King) and Puss in Boots from Shrek

### Baby Hercules
- **Pitch:** Lower than you'd expect for the youngest — slightly husky, toddler warmth
- **Energy:** Cheerful baseline, drops to confused when something unexpected happens (which is always)
- **Delivery:** Slightly slow — he's thinking through what he's saying as he says it. Occasional wrong words used with full confidence.
- **Catchphrase energy:** Simple declarative sentences. "I helped!" (looking at the crater he created)
- **When scared:** Gets very small and very quiet. Almost a whisper.
- **Reference energy:** Young Groot energy — simple but deeply expressive

### Athena
- **Pitch:** Clear, steady, medium for a child's voice — precise articulation
- **Energy:** Calm by default. Rises in precise increments as the situation escalates.
- **Delivery:** Measured. She completes sentences. She does not trail off.
- **The sigh:** Her signature. Three distinct types — the mild disappointment sigh, the "I knew this would happen" sigh, and the rare genuine exasperation sigh.
- **When excited:** The excitement is in the words, not the volume. She speaks faster when she's figured something out.
- **Reference energy:** Hermione Granger at age 7 — knows everything, tells you about it, but you're glad she's there

### Little Perseus
- **Pitch:** Medium, slightly breathy — the voice of someone who is often surprised by events
- **Energy:** Curious and earnest — genuinely interested in everything
- **Delivery:** Slightly hesitant at the starts of sentences when he's uncertain, then gains confidence mid-sentence
- **Asks questions out loud:** Even when no one's answering, he thinks aloud — "Wait, so if the Hydra grows two heads, and we have three swords, and Hercules already broke his sword..."
- **Reference energy:** Young Christopher Robin — gentle, curious, surprisingly brave

### Young Achilles
- **Pitch:** Clear, confident, slightly clipped — a runner's voice, economical
- **Energy:** Cool baseline, ignites when challenged or when speed is involved
- **Delivery:** Short sentences, direct. Not rude — just efficient. Says what he means.
- **The heel topic:** Voice gets very slightly higher and faster when the heel comes up. He hopes no one notices. Everyone notices.
- **Reference energy:** Young Legolas meets competitive Olympic athlete

### Mini Medusa
- **Pitch:** Medium, with a slight hiss on S sounds — not cartoonish, just present
- **Energy:** Starts every scene at "dramatically annoyed," can go to "genuinely threatening" or "accidentally vulnerable"
- **Delivery:** Dramatic proclamations separated by beat-pauses. Likes the word "SILENCE" even when no one is talking.
- **Her snakes:** Each has a tiny distinct voice. Hissy is bossy and loud. Noodle is anxious and fast. Tiny is slow and wise.
- **Reference energy:** Drama kid who's actually good at drama but also kind of exhausting

### Uncle Hades
- **Pitch:** Low, warm, slightly echoing — like he's always in a large stone room
- **Energy:** Tired but not unhappy. The tone of someone who has accepted that this is simply his life now.
- **Delivery:** Deliberate, complete sentences, occasional dry wit delivered completely straight
- **Reference energy:** Gomez Addams as a babysitter — loving and strange and tries his best

### Poseidon
- **Pitch:** Booming but not unpleasant — a voice built for announcing things across a harbor
- **Energy:** Theatrical. Every sentence is a speech.
- **Delivery:** Lots of pauses for effect. Gestures with his voice the way most people gesture with their hands.
- **Reference energy:** Brian Blessed playing a character who knows he's Brian Blessed

## 5. RECURRING LOCATIONS

---

### MOUNT OLYMPUS — THE MAIN HUB
The setting for most episodes. A magical mountain floating above the clouds with a sprawling, beautiful, slightly chaotic community of gods-as-children.

**Key Areas:**

#### The Cloud Courtyard
- Central gathering place — big open area of cloud-stone and grass
- Where the group meets at the start of most adventures
- Has a fountain that only works when Zeus is in a good mood
- Athena's Thinking Bench: a specific stone bench where she does her best planning. The others know not to sit on it.
- The Snack Alcove: an archway where food magically appears. Hercules knows every magic word that makes more food appear. There are seventeen. He's found nine.

#### Zeus's Chamber
- Messy. Athena finds this morally offensive.
- Thunderbolt stand of honor (empty a suspiciously large percentage of the time)
- Lightning marks on the ceiling from accidental indoor zaps
- A window that faces exactly toward Earth — Zeus likes to watch the humans

#### The Hall of Origins
- Grand, ancient, slightly dusty
- Where the Big Rules are kept (written on stone tablets)
- Athena goes here alone sometimes to research
- There is one tablet in the back that nobody can read. It might be important.

#### The Training Ground
- Where Achilles runs (his lap record: 3 seconds around the entire mountain, disputed)
- Where Hercules practices not breaking things (success rate: improving)
- Perseus's obstacle course, which he set up himself and regularly gets lost in

#### The Edge of Olympus
- A cliff where you can look down through the clouds at the human world below
- The group goes here when things are serious
- The view is always beautiful — sunrise over Greece, storms at sea, cities lit at night

---

### THE UNDERWORLD
Uncle Hades's domain. Visited at least twice per season.

**Key Areas:**

#### The Welcome Hall
- Hades greets everyone here with tea
- Cerberus's bed (enormous, very fluffy, takes up half the room)
- The Guest Book — Zeus's entries are the longest and most enthusiastic

#### The Fields of Asphodel
- A grey meadow — not sad exactly, just quiet
- Souls wander here peacefully
- Kids find it slightly eerie but also oddly peaceful
- Good for quiet conversation scenes

#### The Elysium Garden
- The beautiful part of the Underworld — lush, golden-lit
- Hades comes here when he's in a good mood
- The most beautiful flowers grow here because they never need sunlight

#### The River Styx
- Dark water that you cannot touch
- Charon the ferryman is here — very old, very patient, has heard everything
- Running Joke: Charon always comments on whether this is the kids' fifth, sixth, seventh time here. He keeps count. They do not.

---

### THE SEA
Poseidon's domain. Usually visited as an adventure setting.

**Key Features:**
- The sea is alive with personality — dolphins are chatty, fish are opinionated, octopuses are the local gossips
- Poseidon's palace is entirely unnecessary in scale — sixteen ballrooms, only he is ever there
- The sea responds to emotion: calm when Poseidon is calm, dramatic when he's excited

---

### MOUNT OLYMPUS — THE MARKETPLACE
A bustling area of Olympus with stalls, craftspeople, and minor gods going about daily life.

**Key Stalls:**
- HEPHAESTUS's workshop (always has something interesting being made, always extremely loud)
- The ambrosia vendor (Olympus's equivalent of a juice stand)
- Apollo's music corner (he is always there, always performing, the audience is mixed)
- Artemis's arrows shop (she runs it alone, prefers it that way, will not give you a discount)

## 6. RUNNING JOKES

**Per Character:**

### ZEUS
1. **Loses the thunderbolt at least once per episode.** It is always somewhere embarrassing. (Under Hercules. In the fountain. Archie is sitting on it. Medusa accidentally petrified it — briefly.)
2. **His crown falls off at important moments.** He catches it without looking. This eventually stops working.
3. **Says "I have a plan" approximately 1 second before doing the exact opposite of a plan.**
4. **Is weirdly bad at knocking.** Just walks into rooms. This always surprises him when it causes a problem.

### BABY HERCULES
1. **"I HELPED!"** — delivered with total sincerity while surveying significant destruction.
2. **Breaks something, immediately tries to hide it behind something else he also broke.**
3. **Has casually incredible feats mentioned in passing** — "I moved that mountain last Tuesday" — while the thing he was actually asked to do is undone.
4. **Always hungry. Specifically hungry for whatever is most inconvenient to produce right now.**
5. **The accidental flex** — someone tells him something impressive and he immediately (and accidentally) flexes, which breaks his toga strap. He has many spare toga straps.

### ATHENA
1. **Archie looks at the camera when her advice is ignored.** Every time. Never blinks.
2. **Three taps on the chin before any plan.** When things are really serious, it's four taps.
3. **"As I mentioned earlier—"** usually when something she said earlier comes true
4. **Her helmet falls over her eyes at the worst moment.** She never adjusts it until the moment has passed.
5. **Has already solved the problem before the episode starts** but needs the group to get there on their own, which requires patience she is building.

### PERSEUS
1. **Gets lost on the way to places he's been to many times.** His winged sandals don't help — they take him somewhere adjacent to his destination.
2. **Says thank you to inanimate objects and monsters alike.** Completely unconsciously.
3. **His shield shows something important in reflection** right before he consciously realizes it himself.
4. **Pulls something useful from his satchel** that he had "just in case" — the joke is that the items are increasingly specific to the situation.

### ACHILLES
1. **Every mention of his heel:** "It's fine. I'm fine. Everyone stop looking at it." The heel is always fine. In Season 1.
2. **Speed flex goes wrong** — he arrives somewhere so fast he overshoots, has to come back.
3. **Competitive with inanimate objects.** Has raced a waterfall. A cloud. A thrown thunderbolt. His own shadow.
4. **Genuinely doesn't understand why people don't just run everywhere.** "Why are you walking? Walking takes so long."

### MEDUSA
1. **Dramatic exit followed by immediate return** to get something she forgot.
2. **Her snakes argue about the plan mid-monologue.** She has to stop and mediate.
3. **The sunglasses.** There is always a scene where they nearly come off. There is always a scramble.
4. **Genuinely impressive artistic stone garden that everyone admires** — she's trying to stay menacing while accepting compliments.

### UNCLE HADES
1. **Tour offers.** He will try to give a tour. No one wants the tour. He gives it anyway.
2. **The tea.** It's always ready. He always offers it at wildly inappropriate moments. It's always excellent.
3. **Cerberus embarrassments.** The dog is delighted to see visitors. Hades pretends he isn't.
4. **Rivalry with Poseidon.**  Any mention of the sea kingdom gets a very pointed "interesting" and subject change.

### CERBERUS
1. **Left head (Cosmo) growls at everyone first, then waits to see what the other heads do.**
2. **Middle head (Bean) has already decided to love you.** This is not negotiable.
3. **Right head (Dusk) falls asleep at climactic moments.**
4. **All three heads sneeze simultaneously at inopportune times.** The effect is significant.

## 7. STORY RULES & WORLD RULES

### THE RULES OF LITTLE OLYMPUS

**Rule 1: The Gods Are Children, Not Small Adults.**
They have real emotional lives, genuine fears, and are still figuring out their powers. Zeus can't always control lightning. Athena's wisdom is instinct, not experience yet. These are kids who happen to have godlike abilities, not gods who happen to look like kids.

**Rule 2: Problems Are Real But Never Hopeless.**
Monsters are genuinely scary in the moment but always solvable. The solution always requires the whole group — no single character wins alone in Season 1.

**Rule 3: The Lesson Is Earned, Not Stated.**
We never have a character deliver the lesson directly. The lesson emerges from what happens. If we need to land it, one character notices what another character did and names it — "That's why you went back for her, isn't it?" — and the character shrugs and changes the subject.

**Rule 4: Adults Are Helpers, Not Rescuers.**
Hades, Poseidon, Hermes — they can open doors and provide context. They cannot solve the problem for the kids. The kids must solve their own problems.

**Rule 5: Mini Medusa Is Never Truly Defeated.**
She is redirected, outsmarted, or befriended (temporarily). Defeating her would remove the best recurring character. Season 1 ends with her on the path toward being a proper friend — but not there yet.

**Rule 6: The Thunderbolt Has Rules.**
It never hurts anyone. It can zap, scare, light up, or create thunder noises. It cannot be used as a weapon against another character in anger — when Zeus tries this, it fizzles. The thunderbolt responds to intention.

**Rule 7: Hercules's Strength Has Rules.**
He cannot control it perfectly yet. Every episode, he gets one moment of perfect controlled strength — usually at the climax, when he really needs it. This is his arc: learning that control comes from calm, not effort.

**Rule 8: The Underworld Is Not Scary.**
Kids watching should find the Underworld interesting, not frightening. Hades makes it welcoming. The Underworld episodes are often the warmest in the season.

**Rule 9: No Character Is Purely a Villain.**
Including Medusa. Every antagonist has a reason. That reason doesn't excuse bad behavior — but understanding it is part of the lesson.

**Rule 10: Olympus Has Continuity.**
Things that happen in one episode persist. If Hercules broke the fountain in Episode 3, the fountain is still broken in Episode 6 (Hephaestus is "fixing" it). This rewards returning viewers.

### WHAT TRIGGERS AN EPISODE

Most Little Olympus episodes start with one of these:

1. **An Object Is Missing / Broken / Acting Weird** (Zeus's thunderbolt, the fountain, Hades's crown)
2. **A Visitor Arrives** bringing a problem (sea creature, lost human, minor god in trouble)
3. **A Challenge Is Issued** (by Medusa, by a monster, by a misunderstanding)
4. **Someone Tries Something New** and it goes unexpectedly
5. **A Special Event on Olympus** requires preparation (the Games, a celebration, a visitor from another pantheon)

## 11. CONTENT RULES & WHAT WE NEVER DO

### WE ALWAYS DO
- Show fear as normal and courage as choosing to act despite fear
- Resolve conflicts with communication at least as often as action
- Give every character a moment per episode
- Let the "smart" characters be wrong sometimes
- Let the "strong" characters need help sometimes
- Show adults as imperfect but well-meaning
- Include background details that reward rewatch

### WE NEVER DO
- Suggest a child is stupid or incompetent for not knowing something
- Use humiliation as a punchline (embarrassing situations yes, but the laugh is never AT the character)
- Make Medusa purely evil — she always has motivation
- Resolve episodes with "and then a god fixed everything"
- Use the word "stupid" or equivalent
- Make physical appearance the source of a joke about a character (Hercules's strength causing accidents is funny; his appearance is not the joke)
- Show the Underworld as genuinely frightening — it is strange and atmospheric but not scary

### MYTHOLOGY ACCURACY
We take mythology as inspiration, not literal script. When we diverge from myth, we diverge intentionally and consistently. We don't use content from myths that involves violence against children, sexual violence, or adult themes. We adapt freely to serve the audience.

## 12. VISUAL STYLE GUIDE

### Overall Aesthetic
**CoComelon × Disney Junior × Clash of the Titans.**
Bright, saturated, warm. Big eyes on all characters. Expressive faces. Everything slightly rounder and friendlier than real life.

### Color Language
| Character | Primary Color | Secondary | Accent |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Zeus | White / Gold | Electric Blue | Lightning Yellow |
| Hercules | Warm Brown | Lion Gold | Cream/White |
| Athena | Grey-Blue | Silver | White Owl |
| Perseus | Earth Green | Brown | Bronze (shield) |
| Achilles | Sky Blue | Silver | Gold |
| Medusa | Deep Purple | Gold | Snake Green |
| Hades | Black | Deep Purple | Soft Lavender |
| Cerberus | Dark Grey | Ember Red | Bone White |

### Mount Olympus Palette
- Sky: Warm blue with golden-hour quality at all times
- Clouds: Cream / very light lavender
- Stone: Warm white marble with gold veining
- Vegetation: Rich, slightly magical greens and purples

### Character Design Rules
- Eyes are always 1/4 of face height — never smaller
- Hands are slightly large — better for expressive gestures
- Everyone's outfit has a small personal detail that is consistent episode to episode
- Power effects (lightning, strength aura, wisdom glow) are soft and warm, never harsh

### Scene Composition Rules
- Wide establishing shot → medium group shot → close character reactions
- Always at least one visual joke in the background
- Clouds are at character eye level or slightly above — never below. Olympus is IN the sky, not above it.
- The human world (when visible) is always warm and lit — never portrayed as lesser
