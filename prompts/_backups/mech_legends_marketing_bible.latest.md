## BLAZE — Leader · Frontliner

### Character Bio
BLAZE is the smallest mech on the team and the first one through every door. At 8 meters, he stands half the height of ELDER-7 and a third the size of RUMBLE — but no one on the team moves faster, commits harder, or inspires more by example. BLAZE doesn't lead by strategy. He leads by going first, every time, without hesitation, and trusting the team to follow. His amber energy core burns hot at the joints and chest — the brighter it glows, the deeper he's committed. There is no reverse gear in BLAZE.

### Personality Profile
- Instinctive rather than calculated
- Feels the weight of leadership but never shows it in battle
- Genuinely believes the team can win — not because he's analyzed it, but because he's decided it
- Protective of GRANITE specifically — respects his steadiness
- Competitive with STORM — both fast, different styles
- Has never once waited to be asked before acting

### Signature Quotes
- *"We go first. We figure it out on the way."*
- *"The team doesn't follow orders. They follow what I do."*
- *"I've never met an obstacle worth stopping for."*
- *"Smaller than the enemy. Faster than the plan."*
- *"If I fall, GRANITE holds. Get back up."*

### Hero Moments
- EP001: Charges Xerxes's front line alone when the team is still assessing — forces the team to move
- EP003: Takes a direct hit that should end him. Doesn't. Keeps going. The team sees it.
- EP010: Names the insight that changes everything — "Xerxes has never fought a team. He's always fought one."
- EP012: Leads the simultaneous strike. His timing is first. If he's wrong by half a second, everyone dies.

### Strengths
- Fastest ground-to-engagement time on the team
- Unbreakable commitment under pressure
- Natural rally point — the team orients around his position
- Tank alt mode absorbs punishment while maintaining advance
- Reads battlefield momentum in real time

### Weaknesses
- Charges before NOVA finishes calculating
- His confidence can override ELDER-7's warnings
- Smallest physical frame — RUMBLE has to pull him out of close-quarters collapses
- Doesn't retreat well

### Transformation Description
BLAZE's transformation is forward — his chest plates fold down into the tank's hull while his legs become the chassis and his arms lock into track assemblies. The amber core remains visible through the tank's forward grill, pulsing. In tank mode he doesn't slow down. He accelerates. The transformation takes 1.2 seconds. He's already moving before it completes.

### Visual Design Notes
- Hot orange and dark charcoal grey
- Amber energy core glows brighter under stress
- Angular shoulder pauldrons with swept-back fins = visual aggression
- Smallest silhouette on the team — use scale contrast against RUMBLE and ELDER-7
- Facial plate has readable expression — "determined heroic" is the default
- Warm orange glow from all joints in action
- Jet transformation (air mode) available for long-range insertion

### Merchandise Opportunities
- Transforming figure (tank ↔ mech, 2-step conversion)
- LED amber core feature
- "BLAZE CHARGES FIRST" collectible figure in forward-lean pose
- Starter set anchor — most accessible character, entry-level price point
- Backpack / lunchbox with amber core detail
- Mobile wallpaper pack

## GRANITE — Guardian · Defender

### Character Bio
GRANITE doesn't move unless he needs to. When he does, nothing moves him back. Built from geological-inspired layered armor that looks like it grew from the earth, GRANITE is the team's anchor — the one who holds when everything else is pushed back. His cryo-sleeve apparatus on the left arm frosts over when he activates his holding function, a visual signal the team reads instantly: GRANITE is holding. Don't break the line. He runs cold on the outside and warm on the inside, a detail you only learn if you're paying attention.

### Personality Profile
- Doesn't speak unless it matters — when he does, the team listens
- Fiercely loyal, specifically to the team as a unit
- Processes before acting — but once he acts, there's no adjustment
- Closest bond: BLAZE (covers what BLAZE ignores)
- Trusts NOVA's analysis completely; won't move until she confirms
- Finds RUMBLE loud but respects that he gets through

### Signature Quotes
- *"Hold the line. The line is all there is."*
- *"I don't move. That's not weakness. That's the point."*
- *"BLAZE goes first. I make sure there's something to come back to."*
- *"The cryo-sleeve is cold. That means I'm working."*
- *"Every army that's ever lost has moved when they should have held."*

### Hero Moments
- EP004: Xerxes sends a siege force specifically designed to break through. GRANITE holds for 7 minutes alone while NOVA recalculates.
- EP009: The team is being pushed back across the entire line. GRANITE plants, transforms to armored truck, blocks the advance. Single-handedly.
- EP012: Holds through the entire transmission. 4 minutes 17 seconds. The cryo-sleeve is fully frosted by the end. He doesn't release.

### Strengths
- Near-impervious layered armor
- Cryo-sleeve stasis capability — can freeze a zone to prevent advance
- Armored truck mode for terrain control
- Holding function: can absorb and contain signals, force, and pressure simultaneously
- Psychological anchor for the team — his presence changes the calculation

### Weaknesses
- Slowest in open terrain
- Once planted, cannot redirect quickly — STORM has to cover his flanks
- The cryo-sleeve has thermal limits — extended holding causes system heat
- Doesn't adapt mid-hold; NOVA has to redirect the plan around him

### Transformation Description
GRANITE's transformation is a settling — his broad shoulders fold into the armored truck's roof, legs become the heavy chassis, and the cryo-sleeve repositions to the front grille as a ram. The transformation looks like a building deciding to become a vehicle. Slow, deliberate, absolute. In truck mode he's slower but unstoppable. Ramming force exceeds his mech-mode punch by 400%.

### Visual Design Notes
- Slate blue-grey, dark steel, frost white accents
- Cryo-sleeve on left arm — frost builds visibly during holding function
- Geological strata texture on armor — looks like it was cut from a cliff face
- Amber core visible at chest — warm inside, cold outside
- Narrow blue eyes, near-expressionless face — stoic reads as strength
- Size contrast: same height as NOVA and STORM but wider, denser
- Heavy armored vehicle mode: low-profile, maximum surface area

## NOVA — Scout · Strategist

### Character Bio
NOVA processes information the way others breathe — constantly, automatically, at a rate that would destroy less precise systems. Her holographic display panels fold from her forearms and can deploy up to 17 simultaneous data streams, each one a different view of the battlefield. She sees everything. She plans every move. She was built to become a satellite — to orbit, receive, and integrate signals from impossible distances — and suppressed that function for years because it felt like damage. When she stopped suppressing it, 17 channels opened at once. "The three-channel state feels like listening to one part of something. The full state feels like hearing what it's part of."

### Personality Profile
- Speaks in precise, measured sentences — never wastes words
- Processes 17 streams simultaneously but presents one recommendation
- Uncomfortable with incomplete data — will say so
- Closest bond: STORM (speed and stealth = her most precise instruments)
- Deep respect for ELDER-7 — the only archive older than her data
- Underestimated constantly. Prefers it.

### Signature Quotes
- *"I've already run this. There are four scenarios. Three end badly. The fourth requires all six of us acting simultaneously."*
- *"The enemy isn't the problem. The information gap is."*
- *"I don't guess. I calculate. There's a difference."*
- *"Seventeen streams. All active. I can hear the whole thing."*
- *"BLAZE goes on instinct. I go on certainty. We need both."*

### Hero Moments
- EP002: Identifies Xerxes's flanking pattern 4 moves before it happens. Tells STORM exactly where to be. STORM is already there.
- EP005: Opens 17 channels simultaneously for the first time in battle. The team sees all her panels deploy at once. GRANITE stops moving for half a second — first time anyone's seen that.
- EP010: Calculates the only viable scenario — all six simultaneously. Presents it without emotion. The room goes quiet.
- EP012: Integrates the transmission signal across 17 streams and gives it shape. "It's looking for something specific." ELDER-7: "It found it fourteen hours ago."

### Strengths
- 17-stream simultaneous integration
- Satellite transformation for long-range surveillance
- Holographic display panels for real-time team briefing mid-battle
- Pattern recognition that predicts enemy movement
- Can receive and decode signals no other team member can process

### Weaknesses
- Needs full data before committing — BLAZE doesn't wait
- Satellite mode leaves her exposed on the ground
- 17 streams active = thermal load; the cryo-sleeve on her forearms manages it but has limits
- Has never been wrong. Doesn't know how she'll handle it when she is.

### Transformation Description
NOVA's transformation is an unfolding — her body opens outward, panels extending into solar array wings, legs becoming stabilizer fins, and the head system retracting into a sensor dome. In satellite mode she rises and holds position above the battlefield, rotating slowly, feeding 17 streams simultaneously to every team member's HUD. She looks like something built to hear the universe. In hover bike mode, her body compresses into a low aerodynamic profile with the holographic panels folded tight against the chassis, readable only as glowing blue lines in motion.

### Visual Design Notes
- Cold cyan blue and white
- Holographic panels fold from forearms — deploy like wings of light
- Satellite mode: orbital white with cyan sensor arrays
- Multiple sensor arrays on shoulders and head
- Quantum Analysis Matrix core — circular, blue, visible at chest
- Extremely streamlined silhouette — built for speed and reception
- Cryo-sleeve cooling system on forearms (visible thermal management)
- When 17 channels are open: 17 visible holographic panels form a halo around her

### Merchandise Opportunities
- Transforming figure with light-up holographic panel accessories
- "17 STREAMS" deluxe edition with all panels deployed
- Satellite mode display figure
- LED holographic display playset component
- NOVA tactical data card set (collectible)

## STORM — Assassin · Infiltrator

### Character Bio
STORM moves like weather — you don't see it coming until it's already there. Built for lightning-fast strikes and covert operations, STORM's electromagnetic systems arc between joints when active, giving the impression that she's contained electricity rather than metal. Always coiled. Always ready. Her 340% throughput increase when direction-signal engages makes her the fastest data-to-action processor on the team. She doesn't infiltrate enemy positions. She becomes part of the environment and the enemy never knows she was there until the damage is done.

### Personality Profile
- Minimal words, maximum impact
- Observes before engaging — but once she moves, she's committed
- Deep trust in NOVA's analysis — they work as a paired system
- Competitive with BLAZE on speed, but different expression — BLAZE is heat, STORM is precision
- Finds RUMBLE's loudness strategically counterproductive; respects his results
- Doesn't explain herself. The outcome is the explanation.

### Signature Quotes
- *"By the time you see me, I'm already somewhere else."*
- *"Speed isn't the point. Timing is the point."*
- *"NOVA tells me where. I decide when."*
- *"The storm doesn't announce itself."*
- *"I was there. You didn't notice. That was the plan."*

### Hero Moments
- EP006: Infiltrates Xerxes's army undetected, maps the full formation, exits clean. What she finds changes the team's entire strategy.
- EP007: Covers GRANITE's flanks for 4 minutes while he holds — without being detected once
- EP011: Executes a simultaneous three-point strike in 0.8 seconds — one second faster than NOVA calculated as possible
- EP012: Direction-signal engages at full capacity. 340% throughput. She's in four positions before the enemy registers one.

### Strengths
- Motion blur capable — effectively invisible at combat speed
- Stealth coating and EM shielding — radar invisible
- Electrical arcing between joints enhances mobility and can stun proximity targets
- Coiled spring mechanisms = explosive movement from standstill
- Stealth jet alt mode for rapid insertion and extraction

### Weaknesses
- Not built for sustained combat — hit-and-fade only
- No holding capability — GRANITE has to cover after she strikes
- EM arcs become visible under extreme load — compromises stealth
- Operates best with NOVA's data; without it, she's fast but not precise

### Transformation Description
STORM's transformation is the sharpest on the team — joints lock, panels compress, and she collapses into the stealth jet in under 0.5 seconds. The transformation looks like a controlled explosion in reverse. The stealth jet is angular, dark, and produces almost no radar signature. In flight, the EM arcs between the airframe create a visual signature like lightning running through clouds — beautiful and terrifying from a distance.

### Visual Design Notes
- Electric purple and dark silver
- Electromagnetic arcs jump between joints when active
- Coiled posture at rest — spring-loaded appearance
- Stealth jet transformation: sharp angular aircraft, purple EM signature
- Transformation mode icons: stealth, speed, coiled power
- Head close-up shows narrow, focused expression — hunter's stillness
- Motion blur effect in action sequences — she leaves a trail

### Merchandise Opportunities
- Transforming figure with light-up EM arc accessories
- Speed-effect action figure with motion blur base
- "STORM STRIKES" stealth jet with invisible ink reveal
- STORM + NOVA dual pack (paired system)
- Glow-in-the-dark edition (EM arcs)

## ELDER-7 — Sentinel · Support

### Character Bio
ELDER-7 has been watching for 2,800 years. He has seen every mech before this team face down what this team now faces — and he has watched every one of them fall. He carries the archive. He carries the data pad. He carries the weight of knowing exactly how bad this can get and choosing to remain in the fight anyway. His posture is specific: never fully facing forward, always at an angle. Not because he's retreating — because he's always watching something the others haven't seen yet. At 40 meters, he doesn't intimidate through aggression. He intimidates through presence. "Age is not weakness. It is accumulated war."

### Personality Profile
- Speaks rarely; when he does, everyone stops
- Has seen this team's mistakes before, in other teams, in other centuries
- Deeply fond of BLAZE specifically — reminds him of the ones who died well
- Doesn't give orders — gives information, then waits for the team to decide
- His patience is not passivity; it's the patience of someone who has learned what happens when you rush
- Carries one piece of knowledge he hasn't shared: the archive ends. There are no records after what's coming.

### Signature Quotes
- *"I have seen this before. I will tell you how it ends. The choice is whether you want to know."*
- *"Every team before you faced the enemy alone. That's why every team before you is in the archive."*
- *"I'm not behind you. I'm watching the angle you can't see."*
- *"Age is not weakness. It is accumulated war."*
- *"The archive has no entry for this team yet. I intend to write one."*

### Hero Moments
- EP001: Identifies Xerxes's signal before anyone else knows what they're hearing
- EP008: Opens TEAM_FOUR_FINAL_30_SECONDS. Reads it aloud. The team goes quiet for the first time all season.
- EP008: "Every mech in this archive faced the enemy alone. That's why they're in the archive."
- EP012: Updates the archive: "TEAM SIX. RESULT: HELD." The first new entry in 2,800 years.

### Strengths
- 2,800-year intelligence archive
- Orbital drone alt mode for full-battlefield surveillance
- Data pad integration — real-time archive access mid-battle
- Scans for threats invisible to other team members
- His presence alone changes the psychological calculus — enemies know what he's seen

### Weaknesses
- 40 meters tall — not subtle; cannot infiltrate
- Archive is 2,800 years old. Xerxes has adapted.
- Never fully commits to a direction — the angle-watching posture means he's always one step removed
- Carries knowledge that, if shared, might break the team's will. Chooses carefully.

### Transformation Description
ELDER-7's transformation to orbital drone is the slowest on the team and the most dramatic. His enormous frame folds inward, ancient mechanical joints moving with hydraulic deliberateness, until what remains is a disc-shaped orbital platform ringed with ancient sensor arrays and the single blue eye of his scanning system. In drone mode he rises to 200 meters and broadcasts to every team member simultaneously. He sees everything from up there. His data pad remains active throughout.

### Visual Design Notes
- Deep red and black — ancient bronze weathering
- Asymmetric design: left side heavier (battle damage that became structure)
- Data pad always visible in hand
- NEVER FULLY FACING FORWARD — the posture is specific and always maintained
- Ancient inscriptions on armor panels — millennia of records encoded in the body
- Red energy lines through black armor
- One side lit, one side dark in every scene
- Scale: 40 meters — same as COLOSSUS; towering over the team in every group shot
- Calculating expression — intelligent, patient

### Merchandise Opportunities
- Large-scale premium figure (40m scale representation)
- "THE ARCHIVE" collector's edition with data pad accessory and LED eye
- ELDER-7 orbital drone mode display piece
- Ancient inscription armor reveal — hidden text under UV light
- "2,800 YEARS" commemorative edition

## RUMBLE — Bruiser · Demolitions

### Character Bio
RUMBLE doesn't fit. He's louder than STORM wants, slower than NOVA prefers, and larger than BLAZE can plan around. He's also the reason the team is still standing after EP007. RUMBLE doesn't have a holding function, a calculation system, or a surveillance mode. He has six weapon loadouts, a war hammer alt mode, and the singular ability to go through obstacles that everyone else goes around. He's crimson red and he moves like a statement. He spent seasons proving he belonged on this team. Now he's on this team. He's not subtle about what that means to him.

### Personality Profile
- Loud because silence feels like being ignored
- Strategically underestimated — and uses it
- Has read every archive entry ELDER-7 shared. Never mentions it.
- Fiercely protective of BLAZE — sees something of himself in the charge-first approach
- Competitive relationship with GRANITE (immovable object vs. unstoppable force)
- Six weapon loadouts: uses different ones based on what the enemy has built to stop him

### Signature Quotes
- *"The obstacle is the point. If there's no obstacle, what am I here for?"*
- *"NOVA calculates. STORM flanks. I go through."*
- *"Six weapons. I decide which one when I get there."*
- *"Loud and relentless. That's not a flaw. That's a feature."*
- *"The storm always arrives. I make sure they see it coming."*

### Hero Moments
- EP007: Goes in alone, breaks through the outer perimeter of Xerxes's army. Shouldn't have survived. Did.
- EP009: Pulls BLAZE out of a structural collapse with one hand while holding off a flanking unit with the other
- EP011: Switches weapon loadouts mid-engagement three times in 4 seconds. The war hammer finale destroys the forward command position.
- EP012: Transforms to war hammer mode and delivers the kinetic payload that opens the path for the simultaneous strike

### Strengths
- Six interchangeable weapon loadouts (plasma cannon, rail spike launcher, shield generator, scythe blade, missile pod, gravity hammer)
- War hammer alt mode — gravity-enhanced kinetic impact
- Physical size (28m) creates tactical options no other team member has
- Can absorb punishment that would destroy smaller mechs
- Asymmetric design = unexpected attack angles
- Persistent — does not acknowledge the concept of "enough"

### Weaknesses
- Loudest tactical signature on the team — STORM hates working alongside him
- Can't infiltrate; can't hold; can't integrate 17 streams
- Six weapon loadouts means six decision points — sometimes chooses wrong
- Has to be pulled back before he goes too far

### Transformation Description
RUMBLE's transformation to war hammer is controlled destruction — his body mass redistributes into the hammer head, his arm becomes the handle, and the gravity core at the center charges to maximum. The result is 28 meters of compressed kinetic force that swings with enough power to redirect structural formations. When the hammer connects, you hear it before you see it. In hammer mode he's wielded by the team, not self-directed — the only configuration where RUMBLE isn't in control.

### Visual Design Notes
- Deep red and black — crimson red energy lines
- Red energy reactor at chest core
- Asymmetric: left side heavier than right
- Six weapon options shown on character sheet
- War hammer alt mode: massive, gravity-charged, devastating
- Fully forward and confronting posture — never at an angle, always facing the threat
- Calculating expression despite brute-force role — RUMBLE is smarter than he lets on

### Merchandise Opportunities
- Transforming figure with swappable weapon loadouts (all 6)
- War hammer alt mode display piece — largest item in the line
- RUMBLE weapon pack accessory set
- "GOES THROUGH" collector's edition with crimson LED core
- RUMBLE vs. Xerxes battle pack

## XERXES — The Immortal King (S1 Villain)

### Character Bio
Xerxes of Persia doesn't come with an army. He comes with an empire. 2.5 million soldiers. A navy that darkened the ocean. Engineers who could bridge the Hellespont. He is the absolute ruler of the largest empire the ancient world produced, and he doesn't fight to win — he fights because the outcome is already decided. His expression is not anger. It is the composure of someone who has already calculated that you cannot stop what he brings. He has never been truly stopped. He has only been slowed. "The Immortal King" is not hyperbole. It is a warning.

### Villain Profile
- Speaks rarely; lets the scale of his forces communicate
- Never in the front of the battle — always elevated, observing
- Adapts intelligently to whatever stopped him last
- Not cruel for its own sake — cruel because efficiency requires it
- Has faced resistance before. Incorporated it.

### Signature Quotes
- *"There is no strategy that accounts for what I bring."*
- *"300 slowed me for three days. This team has six."*
- *"I have not come to win. I have come to conclude."*
- *"Every empire that fell to me made the same mistake. They thought they could hold."*

### Villain Moments
- EP001: Signal activates. ELDER-7 reads it. Team sees the scale for the first time.
- EP003: BLAZE's solo charge. Xerxes doesn't respond with force. He redirects — and BLAZE finds himself surrounded by something he can't punch.
- EP008: ELDER-7 reads from the archive: every team that faced Xerxes before was destroyed because they faced him alone.
- EP012: The team's simultaneous strike forces retreat — not defeat. "He will come back." ELDER-7 knows this.

### Merchandise Opportunities
