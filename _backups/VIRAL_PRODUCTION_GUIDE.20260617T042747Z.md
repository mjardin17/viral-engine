# VIRAL ENGINE — PRODUCTION GUIDE
*Research-backed rules for making every episode as exciting as possible*
*Last updated: 2026-06-17*

---

## THE ONE RULE ABOVE ALL OTHERS

**Completion rate determines everything.** YouTube's algorithm ranks on how many viewers watch to the end — not raw views. A 30-second Short at 85% completion beats a 60-second Short at 50% every time. Every production decision flows from this.

---

## HOOK ARCHITECTURE (first 3 seconds)

The hook is a single spoken line that runs BEFORE the scene 1 narration. It answers: *"Why do I need to watch this right now?"*

**Hook formula:**
> [Character] + [absurd/impossible situation] + [one unanswered question OR direct consequence]

**Examples from our scripts:**
- "Uncle Poseidon is visiting. He brought a gift. The gift is the entire ocean." (LO_EP007)
- "RUMBLE designed the 14-hour window himself. He wanted to see what they'd do with it." (ML_EP008)
- "Achilles just told Zeus his heel is worse than he's been saying. Full moon is tomorrow." (LO_EP019)

**Rules:**
- Never start with "Welcome back" or a title card
- Never explain the show — start inside the problem
- The hook line is ALSO the thumbnail caption and the Shorts first-frame text overlay

---

## SCENE STRUCTURE (per-scene payoff rule)

Every scene must deliver ONE of these:
- A laugh
- A reveal (something hidden becomes visible)
- A "wait, what?" (expectation broken)
- A status change (character gains/loses something important)

If a scene delivers none of these, it's dead weight. Cut it or merge it.

**Rewatch bait:** Every episode should plant ONE background detail in scene 2–3 that pays off in scene 6–7. Viewers who catch it on rewatch → repeat plays → YouTube ranks it higher.

Examples already in scripts:
- Archie's camera looks (always foreshadow the scene's reveal)
- Zeus's crown (the angle it lists tells you how serious the situation is)
- Athena's three chin taps (only happens when she knows something is wrong)
- RUMBLE's always-watching — appears in a background frame before his move

---

## DUAL-FORMAT PUBLISHING (required for every episode)

Each episode gets cut TWO ways:

### Format A: Full Episode Short (~60s)
- All 7–10 scenes at 9s each
- Title format: `[Character] [did/has/is] [absurd thing] [emoji] | [Show] Episode [N]`
- Ends with "next episode" preview line
- YouTube Shorts upload (≤60s) OR YouTube video (>60s goes to standard upload)

### Format B: Highlight Clip (15–30s)
- The single best scene from the episode
- Opens with the hook line as text overlay
- Ends on a cliffhanger or unresolved laugh — NO resolution
- Caption: "[What just happened] 😂 Wait for the ending... #[Show] #Shorts"
- Uploaded as a SEPARATE Short, 2–3 days after the full episode

**Growth impact:** Channels publishing both formats grow 3x faster than single-format channels.

## THE REWATCH LOOP ENDING

The final scene of every episode should feel like it could loop back to the beginning. Specifically:

- End on a **character expression** — not a title card
- End on an **unresolved thread** — not a full close
- End on the **show's core emotion** — wonder/comedy for LO, tension/respect for ML

**Bad ending:** "And that's what happened! See you next time!"
**Good ending:** [Archie looks at the camera. One slow blink. The Olympus Sea sparkles.]

## RUNNING JOKE MAINTENANCE (LO-specific)

Running jokes are the rewatch engine. They need:
1. **First appearance**: planted without explanation
2. **Second appearance**: audience recognizes it → first laugh
3. **Evolution**: the joke changes slightly each time (not the same bit)
4. **Finale payoff**: the joke resolves into something real

## VILLAIN MANDATE (ML-specific)

**RUMBLE must win or gain something every episode.** Even when the team stops his plan:

- EP005: stopped the Gravity Anchor → **gained** city power grid map
- EP008: tower destroyed → **gained** NOVA's full counter-device specs + BLAZE's gap
- EP012: seal completed → **gained** full team capability data, opened SEASON 2 file

The villain's goal is intelligence, not destruction. He's playing a longer game. Every "defeat" is data collection.

**RUMBLE's intimidation rule**: He should appear in at least one frame per episode that the team doesn't know about. He is always watching.
