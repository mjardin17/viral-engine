## 1. Empire Decoded — Season 3 Topic Candidates

Season 1 (eps 6-15) and season 2 (`topic_backlog`, eps 16-25) are already locked. Below are 10 NEW candidates for season 3 — none overlap with existing topics — each fitting the standing villain-strength brief (antagonist must feel overwhelming).

1. **Marathon: The Run That Saved Greece (490 BC)** — A small Athenian force (with Plataean allies) faces the first Persian invasion of Greece; works as a prequel to the Thermopylae/Salamis arc already covered.
2. **Zama: Hannibal's Final Battle (202 BC)** — Direct sequel to Cannae (S1 ep12). Rome, having rebuilt after its darkest day, finally meets Hannibal on his own continent with Carthage's empire on the line.
3. **Mortgarten: The Swiss Ambush (1315)** — ~1,500 Swiss confederates vs. an ~8,000-strong Habsburg Austrian army; terrain ambush that birthed Swiss military legend.
4. **The Siege of Malta (1565)** — ~6,000 Knights Hospitaller and Maltese militia hold off a ~40,000-man Ottoman invasion fleet for nearly four months.
5. **Lepanto: The Last Great Galley Battle (1571)** — The Holy League's fleet against the "invincible" Ottoman navy, the largest naval battle in the Mediterranean since antiquity.
6. **Myeongnyang: 13 Ships Against 133 (1597)** — Admiral Yi Sun-sin's heavily outnumbered Korean fleet annihilates a massive Japanese invasion fleet using a narrow strait and currents.
7. **San Jacinto: Avenging the Alamo (1836)** — Following the fall of the Alamo, a smaller Texan force led by Sam Houston routs Santa Anna's larger Mexican army in 18 minutes, securing Texas independence.
8. **The Battle of Britain: The Few (1940)** — The RAF, badly outnumbered, stands between an undefeated Nazi war machine that has just conquered most of Western Europe and an invasion of Britain.
9. **Wizna: The Polish Thermopylae (1939)** — ~700 Polish soldiers and a handful of fortified positions hold off a ~40,000-strong German force (with armor and artillery) for three days.
10. **The Six-Day War (1967)** — Israel, surrounded by larger Arab armies massing on multiple fronts, wins a decisive war in less than a week, anchored by a surprise opening air strike.

## 2. New Entertaining Video Series Concepts

Ideas for series distinct from Empire Decoded's "underdog vs. empire" battle format, in the same documentary-narration + AI-generated visuals pipeline style:

- **Myth Decoded** — Greek/Norse/Egyptian mythology retellings as cinematic mini-epics. (Directly builds on the existing "Little Olympus" side project assets already generated.)
- **Invention Decoded** — The dramatic, often accidental origin stories of major inventions (penicillin, the printing press, GPS, etc.), framed as "against the odds" breakthroughs.
- **Heist Decoded** — Real historical heists, cons, and escapes (e.g., the Antwerp diamond heist, Great Train Robbery, Escape from Alcatraz), told with the same tense documentary pacing.
- **Disaster Decoded** — Real survival stories from natural disasters and engineering failures, focused on human decisions under pressure.
- **Mystery Decoded** — Unsolved historical mysteries (Amelia Earhart, the Voynich Manuscript, lost colonies) framed as open investigations rather than battles.
- **Empire Decoded: Naval** — A potential spin-off/mini-arc focused purely on naval battles (Salamis, Lepanto, Myeongnyang, Trafalgar, Midway), since several season 2/3 candidates are already naval-heavy.

Format inspiration drawn from successful short-form history channels: punchy narration, minimal but vivid visuals, strong "stakes" framing in the first 10 seconds.

## 3. General Entertaining Topics List (cross-domain ideas, not yet scoped to a series)

- **History**: forgotten empires, ancient engineering marvels, plagues that changed history, the world's shortest wars, lost cities rediscovered.
- **Science**: near-miss extinction events, the space race's strangest moments, deep-sea exploration, particle physics "what if" scenarios.
- **True crime / mysteries**: famous unsolved codes and ciphers, art heists, disappearances.
- **Nature**: animal "underdog" survival stories (small species outsmarting predators — thematically close to Empire Decoded's hook), migration epics.
- **Pop culture / trivia**: behind-the-scenes stories of iconic films/games, the origin of famous brands and logos, record-breaking feats.

These are broad starting points — happy to develop any of them into a full topic_backlog if you want to spin up a new series.
